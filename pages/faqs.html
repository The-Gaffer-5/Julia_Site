Questions about Urgent and After Hour Care

Does Julia Ronconi provide crisis or urgent treatment?

This office does not provide crisis treatment or after-hours care. If you are experiencing an emergency do NOT call this office. Call 9-1-1 or safely present yourself to the nearest emergency room for immediate care.

What if I am currently experiencing an emergency?

If you are experiencing a life-threatening condition or medical emergency, call 9-1-1. If you are in danger of harming yourself or others, call 9-1-1. If you are experiencing an urgent/crisis situation that is not immediatelyÂ life-threatening, call the HCRS 24-hour crisis line @ 1-800-622-4235

How long do I have to wait for an appointment?

You will be provided the earliest appointment times available. Most patients are scheduled within 1 month of initial inquiry.

Do you take my private insurance?

No. Private insurance is considered out-of-network. Payment is required at the time of service. You will be provided a super bill, which is documentation that you can submit to your insurer for out-of-network reimbursement. Patients are responsible for verifying their out-of-network benefits with their insurance company and for filing claims.

Do you accept Medicare?

Yes. Medicare is accepted. Due to reimbursement issues, Medicare patients must be seen in- person. Telemental health is not available to Medicare patients.

Do you take Medicaid?

No. Medicaid patients will be required to pay out of pocket for treatment. Please note, that as a non-Medicaid provider, prescriptions written by Julia Ronconi will not be covered by Medicaid.

How can I pay for appointments?

Cash, check and credit card are accepted.

What happens if I need to cancel my appointment?

You can cancel or reschedule your appointment up to 24 hours in advance for a full refund. Appointments canceled with less than 24-hour notice are subject to the full visit fee.

Questions about Services Offered

What can I expect at my first visit?

Your first visit will consist of an initial evaluation. During the evaluation, you will be asked questions about your current symptoms, your past psychiatric history, your medical history and other relevant information. These questions may be personal and uncomfortable to talk about or cause discomfort in some people. You are encouraged to be as open as possible so that the most accurate information can be used to help formulate your case.

If you have past medical or psychiatric records that may be helpful, copies of laboratory results or other test results, please bring them to the first visit. Please also have available contact information of your previous psychiatrists or other physicians taking care of you.

How Often Do I Need To Be Seen?

The frequency of appointments is determined through conversations with you and Julia Ronconi but could be as often as every one to two weeks or as long as 90 days and depends on the type of medication you are prescribed, your medical condition, and the stability and complexity of your symptoms. Often, patients are seen more frequently in the beginning stages of treatment. Once stability is reached, the frequency of visits lessens.

Patients who have not been seen for over 6 months will become inactive and are not eligible for medication refills.

If I schedule an appointment, will I have to take medication?

No. The purpose of a psychiatric evaluation is to closely look at all the factors that may be contributing to the difficulty you are experiencing. Once the biological, social, and psychological factors involved and how they are interacting is determined, treatment recommendations will be discussed. Treatment plans including medication may or may not be recommended and you will be actively involved in decisions regarding medications.

Will you work with my existing providers?

Yes, Julia Ronconi is happy to collaborate with your primary care physician, other mental health providers, and/or specialists as needed to provide you with treatment that is specific to your needs.
